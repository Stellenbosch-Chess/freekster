Chess News

Alexander van der Merwe wins Western Cape Chess Championship

Alexander van der Merwe, learner from Paul Roos Gymnasium, won the Western Cape Closed Chess Championship on Sunday 26 September 2021. Van der Merwe scored an impressive 5,5 points out of a possible 6 in his progress to the title. He ensured the title when he defeated the 2019 champion Mark Lewis in the 5th round, who blundered a piece. A draw in the final round against Dione Goredema was enough to ensure a one-point lead over 2020 champion Fide Master Shabier Bhawoodien. Van der Merwe made his intentions clear in the second round of the tournament when he defeated Candidate Master Andreas de Jager in a battle that lasted over four hours.

FM Bhawoodien who won the Paul Roos Gymnasium Open in 2018 and the Western Cape Closed Chess Championship for 2020 suffered his first loss in a tournament held at Paul Roos Gymnasium to Mark Lewis in the second round. He came back strongly with victories in all of his last 4 games. Unfortunately for him, the loss of Lewis to Van der Merwe in the 5th round prevented him from a showdown in the last round against the young champion.

Van der Merwe is not unknown for upsetting senior players. In simultaneous matches he has beaten GM Hikaru Nakamura in 2018. He also boasts draws against GM Wesley So in 2019 and GM Levon Aronian in 2020 in simultaneous exhibition matches. He is also the first winner from the Cape Winelands.

CM Andreas de Jager claimed the bronze medal after defeating his brother, Luan de Jager in the last round.

The tournament attracted players from 5 of the six regions of the Western Cape and strict Covid-protocols had to be adhered to. This did not prevent nearly 90 masked players to battle for the honour to checkmate their opponents.

In the prestige section of the championship, Clayton Macheka from Kraaifontein Club took the honours, with Jiraan Braaf second and Bongolethu Ncethelo taking the bronze medal.

In the Open section, Matie student, Michael van Zyl, secured his gold medal with 5,5 points. Ruan Janse van Rensburg from Mossel Bay won the silver medal, with Shakir Kariem bronze.

Under 13 Boys and Girls Closed Chess Championships for 2021

During a busy chess weekend in the Cape Winelands, Schools Chess held the under 13 Boys Closed Chess Championship for 2021. Raiden Michiel destroyed his opponents in winning all 5 of his games and thereby clinching the title. In the final round he beat fellow Van Wyksvlei learner Jadrian Lelieveld into second position. Van Wyksvlei thereby sealed the first two positions in the tournament. Jadrian Lelieveld ended on 4 out of 5 points. Joining him on 4 points was Cuthanzario Winter, also from Van Wyksvlei. Winter lost out on the silver medal on tie-breaks.

In a tough competition 25 girls competed for the title of the under 13 girls Cape Winelands Closed Chess Champion. When the dust settled it was Anneke Erasmus of Worcester who remained standing. She won all 5 her games and ended with an impressive performance rating of 1639. She was followed by a three-way tie on 4 points for Ancha Groenewald, Grace Hugo and Dell-Maree Nortje. Tie-Breaks again had the last word and placed Ancha Groenewald in second, Grace Hugo in third and Dell-Maree Nortje in fourth position.
